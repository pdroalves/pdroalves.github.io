abstract: 'Data is the gold of the modern era, and the capability of collecting it is as crucial as securely storing and handling it. This is a compilation thesis composed of published or under revision papers that explore different aspects of privacy-preserving computing, such as the efficient implementation of primitives, protocols, and applications. Our work offers a framework for an always-encrypted database, which can store ciphertexts and answer encrypted queries without decryption. In the same direction, we also study the case of large-scale data collection from smart meters. In this case, an entity, such as the electricity provider, collects user data that can be used in statistical methods, such as machine learning, and splits the computation through a network without revealing user information. On the other hand, we also present papers that explore the efficient implementation of the arithmetic used by modern fully homomorphic encryption schemes, such as BFV and the CKKS. We experiment with different methods targeting the CUDA architecture and show how the cryptosystems can be accelerated through the proper choice for the data structure, locality, and algorithm used on the polynomial multiplication. Four papers are presented on these topics, as well as a discussion that connects our work during the years of research.'
