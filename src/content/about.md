I'm a Senior GPU Engineer at [Zama](https://www.zama.ai/), where we work to make fully homomorphic encryption (FHE) practical by shipping it in production-grade systems—including [TFHE-rs](https://github.com/zama-ai/tfhe-rs) and [FHEVM](https://docs.zama.ai/fhevm).

I have a PhD in Computer Science from the [Institute of Computing](https://www.ic.unicamp.br/) at the [University of Campinas](https://www.unicamp.br/) (Unicamp), and I care deeply about privacy-preserving technologies and secure cloud computing. I've been building on [CUDA](https://developer.nvidia.com/cuda-toolkit) since the early days of that stack. I'm still learning, but by now I can usually tell whether the slowdown is the math, the memory, or me having a bad day.

My research focuses on developing efficient implementations of cryptographic schemes, particularly homomorphic encryption, to enable computation over encrypted data without compromising privacy. I have worked extensively with GPGPU implementations to accelerate those cryptographic operations.

I am passionate about open-source software and believe in making privacy-preserving technologies accessible to everyone.
